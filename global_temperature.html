Climatological data has been collected in various forms for hundreds of years. The data used to develop the prediction models on this page dates back to 1880. The globe to the right displays the climate stations that have been collecting data since 1880 or before.




"The September 2020 globally averaged temperature departure from average over land and ocean surfaces was the highest for the month of September in the 141-year NOAA global temperature dataset record, which dates back to 1880."

National Centers for Environmental Information, Assessing the Global Climate in September 2020

The largest global temperature rise happened at the time of the Industrial Revolution, but temperature has continued to rise throughout the 21st century. The regression model used here uses the trend of the past 40 years to predict the continued climb if nothing is done to change (the Projection trend line). The Easing rules trend line depicts the change if an addtional 0.05% CO2 were allowed each year. The Stricter rules trend line depicts the change in Global Temperature if CO2 emmissions were decreased 0.05% each year.

How does the internet think?

On Twitter, users share opinions on multitudes of various topics, not excluding the issue of climate change. Two tags on climate change are particularly opinionated in opposite ways:
#climatechangeisreal and #climatechangehoax.

We compiled lists of tweets (with Twint) for each tag from January 2014 to October 2020 that were retweeted at least 100 times.
We then used the module fastAI to create a neural network and perform deep learning on each group of tweets. With this model, we were able to generate new tweets based on the existing ones.
