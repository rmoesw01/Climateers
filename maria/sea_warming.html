at the north pole

Shrinkage of Northern Sea Ice


Sea ice at the North Pole is rapidly dwindling.

GeoTIFFs from NSIDC, Sea Ice Index, Sept. 1980-2020

The average extent of northern sea ice has changed dramatically since 1980 as seen in the corresponding figure. Hover over the sea ice (depicted in white) to view the numerical measure of the area. The interactive globe can also be dragged and zoomed out.

This is consistent with rising global temperatures, both atmospheric and oceanic. This also directly contributes to rising sea levels, which in turn leads to flooding and higher likelihood of hurricane formation.

GeoTIFF files of September sea ice extent every 5 years were obtained from the NSIDC, polygonized with QGIS, and further manipulated with mapshaper to return files in geoJSON format.
